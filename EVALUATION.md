# 📊 Project Evaluation & Selection

> **Purpose:** Score all 100 projects, rank them, and select the top 25 for full proposal development.
> **Scoring Scale:** 1–10 per criterion
> **Selection Threshold:** Top 25 by composite weighted score

---

## Evaluation Criteria & Weights

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Innovation | 12% | Originality of the idea and approach |
| Research | 10% | Depth of research contribution and publishability |
| Engineering Complexity | 15% | Architectural sophistication and technical challenge |
| Software Engineering | 12% | SE practices, patterns, and professional-grade development |
| AI Integration | 8% | Meaningful and purposeful use of AI/ML |
| Difficulty | 10% | Implementation challenge calibrated for a student team |
| Sponsor Potential | 8% | Likelihood of corporate or institutional sponsorship |
| Startup Potential | 8% | Commercial viability as a product or service |
| Implementation Risk | 7% | Risk of failure due to data, complexity, or unknowns |
| Educational Value | 10% | Breadth and depth of skills acquired |

> **Note:** Implementation Risk is scored inversely — a score of 10 means LOW risk (favorable), and 1 means VERY HIGH risk (unfavorable).

## Selection Justification

### Why These 25?

The selected projects satisfy the following criteria simultaneously:

1. **Domain Diversity:** 13 distinct engineering domains represented — no single domain dominates.
2. **Engineering Depth:** Average Engineering Complexity score of 8.6/10 — no trivial projects.
3. **Feasibility:** All projects are achievable by a team of 4–6 students in 8–10 months with proper planning.
4. **Industry Relevance:** Average Sponsor Potential of 7.9/10 — real companies care about these problems.
5. **Educational Value:** Average Educational Value of 9.0/10 — students will grow significantly.
6. **Balanced AI Integration:** AI is meaningful where present but never dominates the engineering challenge.

### Notable Exclusions

| Project | Score | Why Excluded |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Cloud-Native Service Mesh (#5) | 7.05 | Too similar to Container Orchestration Engine (#1). Keeping the more ambitious project. |
| Multi-Region Replication (#10) | 6.92 | Overlaps with Distributed Key-Value Store (#28). The KV store is more educational. |
| SIEM System (#15) | 6.96 | Strong but overlaps with both Distributed Log Analytics (#91) and Zero-Trust (#11). |
| Deception IDS (#13) | 7.01 | Good project but niche. Automated Pen Testing (#14) has broader educational value. |
| Stream Processing Framework (#33) | 7.01 | Conceptually overlaps with Data Lakehouse (#27) which is more complete. |
| Time-Series DB Engine (#31) | 7.45 | Very close to cutoff. Overlaps with KV Store (#28) in the "build a database" category. |
| Supply Chain Visibility (#78) | 7.03 | Just below cutoff. Strong industrially but lower research depth. |
